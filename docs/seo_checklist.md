My FULL On-Page SEO Checklist

(Lessons from ranking 100s of pages)

—

#1. Title Tag Structure:

- Put keyword at start (Google weighs early words more)
- Keep under 60 characters (avoid truncation)
- Add year, numbers, or power words

✅ "SEO Checklist 2026: 21-Step Guide to Ranking Higher"
❌ "The Ultimate and Complete Guide to Search Engine Optimization"
—

#2. Meta Description:

- Use main keyword naturally in first sentence
- Add strong call-to-action at the end
- Keep between 155-160 characters

✅ "Want higher rankings? Follow our proven on-page SEO checklist to optimize your content the right way. Includes step-by-step tutorial + free template."
❌ "Learn about SEO and how to do SEO for your website with our SEO guide that teaches SEO optimization techniques for better SEO rankings."
—

#3. Content Structure:

- H1: Main keyword + clear value
- H2s: Use related keywords (no forced stuffing)
- H3s-H6s: Break down each section

Example:

- H1: "On-Page SEO Checklist (Step-by-Step Guide)"
- H2: "1. Title Tag Optimization"
- H3: "How to Write SEO-Friendly Titles"
  —

#4. URL Structure:

- Keep it short and clean
- Include target keyword
- Use hyphens between words
- Don't include the year

✅ /on-page-seo-checklist/
❌ /2026/01/ultimate-guide-to-on-page-search-engine-optimization-tips-and-tricks/
—

#5. Image Optimization:

- Descriptive file names (keyword-image.jpg)
- Alt text with target keywords
- Compress all images

✅ Alt: "on-page SEO checklist template example"
❌ Alt: "img_89271.jpg"
—

#6. Internal Linking:

- Link to 3-5 relevant posts
- Use keyword variations as anchor text
- Link to high-priority pages

✅ "Check our [technical SEO guide] for more tips"
❌ "Click [here] to learn more"
—

#7. Content Optimization:

- Include keyword in first 100 words
- Break paragraphs into 1-3 sentences
- Use bullet points for scannability
- Check top 10 results' length
- Make yours 1.5x more detailed

✅ 2,500 words of actionable content
❌ 500 words of surface-level info
—

#8. User Experience + Technical Checks:

- Add table of contents
- Use descriptive subheadings
- Include visual breaks (images/charts)
- Fix broken links
- Check mobile responsiveness
- Optimize page speed

✅ Under 3s load time, easy to scan
❌ Slow loading, hard to read
—

On-page SEO isn't complicated, but most people skip the basics.

- Start with title tags and meta descriptions
- Structure your content properly
- Optimize images and internal links
- Focus on user experience
- Check the technical details
